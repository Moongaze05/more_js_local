He's got some of the best old silver in England.' The other man shook his head and turned so I could see his face. I saw him say: 'Old silver is no better than new. You can only melt it.' The first man's face became red. 'Only melt it! Don't be stupid! I can sell old silver at good prices. And that silver in Myrtle Cottage must be worth more than a thousand pounds. There's a silver salt-cellar worth at least a hundred.' The other man looked at me while I was watching his friend speak. He had fair hair and blue eyes. 'That child is watching us,' he whispered. 'Be careful.'
